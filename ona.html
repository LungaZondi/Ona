The Name I Gave You

An ornament isn't just decoration — it's the thing you handle with the most care. The thing you keep coming back to. The thing that, if it fell, you'd feel it. That's you, Ona. Clumsy and brave and somehow the most delicate thing I know. You bump into every corner life puts in front of you and keep going anyway — and I think that's one of the most quietly beautiful things I've ever watched someone do.

Three years of silence is a long time. A long time to carry something. And then one day the silence just — ended. And here we are. I'm glad we're here.

The Princess Chronicles

Seven names I've called you
& why every one fits

Because every morning I wake up and a different princess comes to mind. This is me explaining myself.

🌿 01

Rapunzel

The one who waited & still bloomed

Rapunzel spent years locked away from the world, and instead of shrinking she grew — filled her tower with art and colour and herself. When she finally got out she didn't run from life, she sprinted toward it with terrifying hope. That energy you carry — that willingness to still want things after hard seasons — that's Rapunzel.

"You waited out the quiet and came back luminous. That's not small."

🌸 02

Fiona

The one who is more than one thing

Everyone always underestimates Fiona. They see the surface and miss the depth. She's complex, surprisingly funny, fiercely loyal, and deeply tender underneath the armour. She doesn't need to be rescued — she just needs someone to actually look. You're Fiona because you are so much more than what people catch at first glance.

"Layers and layers, and every one worth knowing."

✨ 03

Cinderella

The one with grace in hard circumstances

Not for the glass slipper — for the part where she kept her softness intact through everything that tried to take it from her. There's something almost defiant about staying gentle when the world gives you reasons not to. You do that. You stay you. That's harder than it looks.

"Grace that costs something is the only kind that matters."

🌊 04

Moana

The one who goes anyway

Moana had every reason to stay where it was safe. She went anyway. Not recklessly — she was terrified. But the pull toward something true was stronger than the fear. The distance between us has never stopped you from being present. That kind of courage is exactly Moana's. You don't let geography win.

"The ocean couldn't stop her. Miles can't stop you either."

🌙 05

Aurora

The one whose calm is magnetic

Sleeping Beauty's whole thing is this inexplicable quality — everyone who meets her just wants to be around her, and she doesn't even try. She radiates something peaceful. That's the version of you I think you don't always see: the one who makes a room feel easier to be in just by being in it.

"You calm things down without knowing you're doing it."

🏹 06

Merida

The one who refuses to be handled

Merida is stubborn in the exact right way. She knows her own mind. She doesn't perform for anyone. When she's wrong she figures it out herself and comes back with a full heart. She's real in a way that most people aren't. That's you on the days you push back on me. Honestly? Good. Don't stop.

"I don't want easy. I want you, exactly how you are."

🌟 07

Tiana

The one who builds something real

Tiana is the most grounded princess there is. She dreams, but she also works. She loves deeply but never loses herself in it. She earns what she has and holds onto it with both hands. There's a version of you that knows exactly what she wants from life and is already quietly building it. That version is Tiana. She's the one I respect most.

"You know what you're made of. I think you always have."

On Distance

Miles are real.
So is this.

Long distance has its own kind of weight. It's the 3am math of time zones. The way a day can feel so full and then you realise the person you most wanted to tell wasn't there for any of it.

But here's what distance can't touch: the fact that you're still the first person I think to tell things to. That hasn't changed across three years of silence or a thousand kilometers of geography.

And yes — I do get a little territorial when you bring up other guys. I'm not going to pretend I don't. It's not about control, it's just — you matter, and some things remind me of that more sharply than I expect. You can tease me about it. I probably deserve it.

yes, the jealousy is a you problem — a you-matter-a-lot problem

A small letter,
for the record

I don't say this to make you feel any particular way. I say it because it's true and I've learned that leaving true things unsaid is a waste.

You are the most specific kind of wonderful — not a general, vague wonderful that could apply to anyone, but the specific kind that only makes sense when it's you. The way you're clumsy and then laugh at yourself. The way you keep bumping into the world and the world somehow doesn't manage to make you hard.

Three years is a long gap. And somehow you come back into a conversation and nothing about why I care has moved. That's not nothing, Ona. That's something I don't take lightly.

You're my ornament. The kind you don't put in storage. The kind you keep out, where you can see it.

Always careful with you,
always.
